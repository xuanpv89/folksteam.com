Compassio is a wellbeing and emotional literacy project connected to the Folks C.H.E.S.S model. It brings together psychology-informed content, podcast and video learning, events, expert booking, publications, and the Emotional Compass card deck.

This guide turns the Compassio content system into a usable operating map: what to read or watch first, how to use the Emotional Compass, when to join an event, and when to book expert support.

<Aside type="tip" title="Source themes">
  The Compassio site centers on mental healthcare, career, love and relationships, self development, emotion, personal perspective, podcast/video, expert booking, events, publications, and content collaboration.
</Aside>

## Start Here

<Steps>
  1. **Choose the topic you are working with.** Start with one area: mental healthcare, career, love, self development, emotion, or personal perspective.
  2. **Watch or read one Compassio piece.** Use the podcast and article library as the Content layer. Do not try to consume everything at once.
  3. **Name the emotional signal.** Use the Emotional Compass deck or a simple reflection note to identify the feeling, trigger, message, and possible response.
  4. **Move into conversation.** Bring the insight into a check-in, coaching session, classroom, team reflection, or community discussion.
  5. **Choose the next action.** End with one small behavior: a conversation to initiate, a boundary to clarify, a question to ask, or a support request to make.
</Steps>

## Featured Compassio Content

These pieces from Compassio can be used as learning modules or discussion prompts:

- **Goc Vuong:** From "Love or not love your colleague?" to "Should leadership be built on love or discipline?" Use it for workplace care, leadership style, and boundaries.
- **Cai Nghe Cai Nghiep:** "What does love have to do with career choice?" Use it for motivation, career direction, and the emotional side of work.
- **Relation Ship:** "What is love? Love and things that look like love." Use it to clarify intimacy, passion, commitment, attachment, and misrecognition.
- **Hoc Nhanh, Song Cham:** Active listening practice. Use it as a short exercise before coaching, team review, or conflict conversation.
- **Doc Cung Compassio:** "Happy Alone." Use it for solitude, loneliness, self-connection, and the difference between avoidance and chosen alone time.
- **Workshop Thi & Ca:** Creative lyric-writing experience. Use it when the goal is to turn reflection into creative expression.

## How to Use La Ban Cam Xuc

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="For personal reflection" icon="pencil">
    Draw or choose one card. Write down the emotion, the situation that activated it, the body signal, the need underneath it, and one gentle next action.
  </Card>
  <Card title="For team check-ins" icon="seti:yaml">
    Invite each person to choose a card that matches their current state. Keep sharing bounded: one feeling, one sentence of context, one thing the group should know.
  </Card>
  <Card title="For workshops" icon="rocket">
    Use the cards to open a session, deepen a discussion, or close with reflection. Pair cards with journaling, pair sharing, or small-group synthesis.
  </Card>
  <Card title="For coaching" icon="star">
    Use a card as a doorway into the client's language. Ask what the emotion protects, what it requests, and what action would respect both the feeling and the goal.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Suggested Session Flow

Use this structure for a 45 to 60 minute Compassio-style reflection session:

1. **Opening check-in:** Everyone chooses one word or card for their current state.
2. **Content prompt:** Watch, read, or summarize one Compassio topic.
3. **Reflection:** Each person writes what felt true, difficult, or surprising.
4. **Emotional mapping:** Identify the feeling, signal, message, and reaction.
5. **Conversation:** Share in pairs or small groups with active listening.
6. **Action:** Choose one next step that is small enough to do within a week.

## C.H.E.S.S Implementation Notes

- **Content:** Use articles, podcasts, videos, and creative prompts as the discovery layer.
- **Hub:** Create a group, class, team space, or community thread where reflections can continue.
- **Event:** Run workshops, talks, lives, or reflection circles around one topic at a time.
- **Store:** Offer cards, publications, templates, and guided exercises as repeatable tools.
- **Session:** Add expert booking, coaching, or small-group mentoring for deeper support.

<Aside type="caution" title="Important boundary">
  Compassio content and Emotional Compass practices support awareness, conversation, and learning. They are not a replacement for clinical care when someone needs professional mental health treatment.
</Aside>
