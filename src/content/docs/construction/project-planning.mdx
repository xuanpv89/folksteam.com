A good Compassio session is simple, warm, and structured. It gives people enough safety to notice what they feel, enough language to name it, and enough direction to choose one next action.

<Card
  title="A Compassio session plan includes:"
  icon="seti:notebook"
>
  - One theme: mental healthcare, career, love, self development, emotion, or perspective
  - One content prompt: podcast, article, video, or Emotional Compass card
  - One reflection method: journaling, pair sharing, group check-in, or creative response
  - One closing action: request, boundary, question, or next experiment
</Card>

## Planning Steps

<Steps>
  1. **Define the emotional objective.** Decide whether the session is about awareness, communication, decision-making, repair, creativity, or support.
  2. **Choose the source material.** Pick one Compassio podcast, video, article, card, or event theme.
  3. **Set the facilitation boundary.** Tell people what the session can and cannot hold.
  4. **Design the sharing rhythm.** Use private reflection before group sharing.
  5. **Close with a doable action.** The end of a session should feel grounded, not emotionally open-ended.
</Steps>

## Example Structures

- **15 minutes:** one-card check-in, one sentence of context, one support request.
- **30 minutes:** content prompt, journaling, pair-share, one next action.
- **60 minutes:** opening card, theme introduction, guided reflection, group synthesis, action commitment.
- **90 minutes:** deeper workshop with multiple rounds, creative response, and closing support resources.
