Compassio work can touch sensitive feelings. Safety does not mean avoiding depth; it means creating enough clarity, consent, and pacing for people to participate without pressure.

<Card title="Core safety commitments">
  - Participation is invitational, not forced.
  - People own their private notes and personal stories.
  - Facilitators reflect and ask; they do not diagnose.
  - Sharing has time boundaries and emotional boundaries.
  - Professional support is recommended when the topic needs clinical care.
</Card>

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Before a session" icon="star">
    Name the topic, expected depth, and opt-out options. Prepare a lighter prompt for people who do not want to share personal stories.
  </Card>
  <Card title="During a session" icon="seti:yaml">
    Slow the pace when emotions intensify. Use active listening and avoid fixing, debating, or interpreting too quickly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="After a session" icon="approve-check-circle">
    Close with grounding, next steps, and support options. Do not leave participants emotionally activated without a landing point.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Aside type="caution" title="Clinical boundary">
  Compassio tools support awareness, conversation, and education. They are not a substitute for therapy, crisis support, or clinical diagnosis.
</Aside>
