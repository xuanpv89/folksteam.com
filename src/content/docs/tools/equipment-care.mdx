Compassio tools are simple, but the way they are handled matters. Cards, prompts, notes, and workshop materials should protect trust, privacy, and emotional safety.

<Card title="Core care principle" icon="approve-check-circle">
  Treat every reflection tool as a doorway into someone's inner world. Keep it clean, organized, optional, and consent-based.
</Card>

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Card deck care" icon="star">
    - Keep La Ban Cam Xuc cards dry, clean, and easy to access.
    - Return cards to the deck after each session.
    - Avoid writing private participant notes directly on shared cards.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Prompt care" icon="pencil">
    - Use one clear prompt at a time.
    - Avoid prompts that push people to reveal more than they choose.
    - Keep prompts specific enough to create action.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Note care" icon="document">
    - Let participants own their private notes.
    - Do not collect personal reflections without consent.
    - Summarize group patterns without naming individuals.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Session care" icon="seti:yaml">
    - Open with consent and boundaries.
    - Close with grounding and one next step.
    - Offer support links when the conversation becomes heavy.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Storage and Reuse

- Store card decks in a stable place so facilitators can access them quickly.
- Keep session templates separate from personal participant notes.
- Review prompts after each session and remove any that felt confusing, forcing, or too abstract.
- Build a small library of tested prompts for mental healthcare, career, love, self development, emotion, and perspective.
